Established in 1950 by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA), Formula 1 has evolved into a global phenomenon, captivating audiences around the world with its blend of speed, skill, and engineering prowess. At its core, Formula 1 is a single-seater, open-wheel racing series that features a calendar of Grand Prix races held across diverse tracks, from traditional circuits like Monza and Silverstone to city-based street circuits like Monaco and Singapore. Renowned names such as Scuderia Ferrari, Mercedes-AMG Petronas Formula One Team, and Red Bull Racing have etched their legacies in the annals of the sport, contributing to its rich tapestry. Iconic figures such as Juan Manuel Fangio, Ayrton Senna, Michael Schumacher, and more recently, Lewis Hamilton, have become synonymous with greatness, their exploits etching indelible marks on the sport's history. The sport's commitment to sustainability is increasingly evident, with initiatives focused on reducing carbon footprints, exploring alternative fuels, and promoting eco-friendly practices.
